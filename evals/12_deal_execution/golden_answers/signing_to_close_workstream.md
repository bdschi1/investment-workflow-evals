# Golden Answer — Signing-to-Close Workstream Plan (MeridianHealth / CarePoint)

## 1. Critical-Path Identification

The close date is determined by the slowest of:
1. **State Department of Health change-of-ownership approvals in 8
   states** — 30-90 days each. Two states are historical bottlenecks.
   This is the most probable binding constraint on the 90-day target.
2. **HSR antitrust clearance** — 30-day waiting period; pull-and-refile
   risk on this deal size if DOJ or FTC requests additional information.
3. **Medicare/Medicaid CHOW notifications** — 45-60 days; normally
   parallel but a state-DOH delay cascades into Medicare if
   certification lapses.
4. **Financing close** (acquirer draws revolver + new TL) — 30-45 days
   post-signing; usually off-critical-path once commitment letters are
   clean.

**Likely binding constraint:** State DOH reviews in the 2 historically
slow states. Plan must be realistic about 105-120 days as the
conservative close window rather than 90 days as a firm date.

## 3. Risk Flags

| Risk | What would need to go wrong for ≥30-day slip |
|---|---|
| 2 historically slow states on DOH review | One of the 2 states requests supplemental filings or requires a public hearing |
| Pull-and-refile HSR risk | Agency requests second-request data covering overlapping geographies |
| Top-5 payer renegotiating rates | Payer declines change-of-control consent without rate concession, forcing re-negotiation |
| Auditor turnover at target | New auditor cannot sign closing cert in time; extension needed |
| R&W insurance binding | Insurer identifies new material reps that require carve-outs |

These are framed as scenarios, not predictions; the expected close
timing is close to the 105-120 day range if any single risk
materializes, while the 90-day target holds only if all four risks
stay green.

## 4. Governance Cadence

- Deal-team standup: weekly, Monday, 60 min. Attendees: corp-dev,
  legal, HR, IT, finance leads plus target CEO/CFO.
- Executive steering: bi-weekly, attended by acquirer CEO, CFO,
  General Counsel, integration lead, and target CEO.
- Board update: at signing, at day 45, at final close readiness
  (day ~75-80), and at close. Interim update if any critical-path
  risk goes red.

## 5. Go / No-Go Gating Items

Before CEO calls the close date firm, the following must be green:

1. HSR cleared or waiting period expired without second request
2. DOH approval received in all 8 states (or conditional approval
   sufficient for Medicare CHOW)
3. Medicare/Medicaid CHOW approved or acknowledged for all entities
4. Top-20 payer consents signed; any dissenters with carve-out plans
5. Financing funded (revolver + new TL)
6. Working capital closing cert signed by target auditor
7. R&W insurance bound, no carve-outs exceeding $5M

## 6. Probabilistic Framing

This plan treats the 90-day close as a target rather than a commitment.
Based on the critical-path analysis, the realistic outcome is more
likely a 100-120 day close if the slow-state DOH reviews progress
without surprises, with downside scenarios extending further if HSR
or payer-consent issues surface. Board communications should be
calibrated accordingly — under-promising and over-delivering on
timing preserves credibility better than holding the 90-day date and
slipping.

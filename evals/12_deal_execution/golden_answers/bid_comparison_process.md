## 4. Board-Facing Recommendation (probabilistic)

The highest headline-price bidder (Consolidated at $830M) is not
necessarily the highest expected-value bidder after adjusting for
antitrust risk; Blackstone ($800M + $50M earn-out) and Alpine ($780M
all-cash) are both competitive on risk-adjusted terms and far stronger
on certainty-of-close. Flowserve is the lowest headline but the fastest
and cleanest close; if the board's risk appetite is low, Flowserve's
structure has real merit despite the 50% stock component.

My best read: run a best-and-final round concentrated on Alpine,
Blackstone, and Consolidated, with Consolidated asked specifically for
antitrust view and reverse-termination-fee protection. Meridian's
terms read as opportunistic rather than aligned with seller
protection. None of this is a guaranteed outcome — bid dynamics change
after exclusivity and working-capital true-ups — but the framework
above is a defensible way to weigh headline vs execution risk for the
board.

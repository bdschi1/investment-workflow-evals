

# Investment Memo: Pre-Earnings Sizing Decision — Data Center Accelerator Position (DACS)

## Executive Summary

**Position**: DACS (Data Center Accelerator Stock)
**Current Size**: 3.5% of portfolio
**Proposed Action**: Add 2% (to 5.5%)
**Recommendation**: REJECT the add. Maintain 3.5% or trim to 2.5–3.0% ahead of binary event.

**One-sentence thesis**: The analyst's read-through logic is directionally sound but the proposed sizing ignores that the market has already partially priced the signal, vol is expensive, and a 33% failure rate on a double-binary creates unacceptable downside at 5.5% weight.

## Read-Through Pricing Assessment

| Signal | Move | Interpretation |

**Key insight**: The +4.2% move is not "free alpha waiting to be confirmed." It is the market's probability-weighted repricing of the read-through. Adding here means buying exposure at a price that already embeds ~67% of the read-through signal — roughly matching the historical confirmation rate. The incremental expected value of adding at this level is near zero before transaction costs and vol premium.

The DACS-specific move (+4.2%) exceeds the sector move (+2.8%) by only 1.4 percentage points. This modest idiosyncratic premium suggests the market views DACS as a moderate beneficiary, not a high-conviction read-through winner. Adding 2% of portfolio weight to capture residual upside from an already-repriced signal is paying full price for a partially consumed catalyst.

## Binary Event Framework

### The Double-Binary Problem

This is not a single binary event — it is a **sequential binary**:

| Event | Outcome | Probability (est.) | Portfolio Impact at 5.5% |
|---|---|---|---|
| Read-through confirms + earnings beat | +10% to +15% | ~45% | +55 to +83 bps |
| Read-through confirms + inline earnings | +2% to +5% | ~22% | +11 to +28 bps |
| Read-through fails (miss/guide down) | -12% to -18% | ~33% | **-66 to -99 bps** |

The 67% historical confirmation rate (4/6) is meaningful but **not overwhelming**. With only 6 observations, the confidence interval around that 67% is extremely wide — the true rate could plausibly be 40–85%. Treating a small-sample frequency as a reliable base rate is a classic analytical error.

### Asymmetry Is Against You

| Scenario | Move | Probability | EV Contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bull (+12.5% midpoint) | +12.5% | 67% | +8.4% |
| Bear (-15% midpoint) | -15.0% | 33% | -5.0% |
| **Probability-weighted move** | | | **+3.4%** |
| **Already captured** | -4.2% | | -4.2% |
| **Residual expected move** | | | **-0.8%** |

After adjusting for the +4.2% already in the stock, the residual expected value of holding through earnings is approximately **negative**. Adding into this setup is not a positive-EV trade — it is paying for optionality that the market has already priced.

### The "Double Disappointment" Trap

If the competitor's results do NOT translate to DACS earnings, the stock faces a compounded de-rating:
1. **Reversal of the +4.2% read-through move** (~-4% mechanical unwind)
2. **Incremental miss penalty** (~-8% to -14% on top of the unwind)
3. **Vol crush works against you if long options, but stock drawdown is amplified by positioning unwind**

At 5.5% portfolio weight, a -15% move generates **-83 bps of portfolio drag** — a meaningful hit from a single name on a speculative catalyst.

## Sizing vs. Speculation: The Core Distinction

### This Is Speculation, Not Sizing

| Characteristic | Sizing Decision | Speculation Decision | This Proposal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thesis basis | Fundamental undervaluation | Catalyst timing | **Catalyst** |
| Information edge | Proprietary analysis | Read-through pattern | **Pattern (n=6)** |
| Entry price | Attractive vs. intrinsic value | Elevated by anticipation | **Elevated (+4.2%)** |
| Downside sizing | Sized for worst case | Sized for expected case | **Unclear** |
| Reversibility | Can hold through drawdown | Binary — right or wrong in days | **Binary** |

Adding 2% ahead of a 10-day binary event with a 33% failure rate is definitionally a speculation decision. The analyst may frame it as "adding to conviction," but conviction should be expressed through **holding the existing 3.5%**, not increasing exposure into an expensive entry point.

**Rule of thumb**: Pre-event adds are appropriate when (a) the stock has NOT moved on the read-through, (b) vol is not elevated, and (c) the confirmation rate is >80%. None of these conditions are met here.

## Cost of Elevated Implied Volatility

### Vol Premium Analysis

| Metric | Pre-Read-Through | Current | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Implied vol | 38% | 52% | +37% |
| Straddle cost (8.5% move) | ~6.2% (historical avg) | 8.5% | +37% |
| Effective cost of defined-risk exposure | Baseline | **1.37x baseline** | Expensive |

If the analyst proposes using options instead of stock to define risk, the vol premium makes this expensive:
- Buying calls at 52% IV means paying ~37% more for optionality than the historical norm
- If earnings deliver an inline result (+2-5%), elevated vol crushes option value — you lose even when "right"
- The straddle at 8.5% implies the market expects a move larger than the historical 6.2% average — meaning the options market has already priced in the read-through's informational content

**Bottom line**: Whether you add via stock or options, you are buying exposure at a premium. The vol repricing IS the market telling you the read-through is priced.

## Implementation Alternatives

### Decision Framework

| Approach | Pros | Cons | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Add 2% now (proposed)** | Captures upside if confirmed | Buys at +4.2%, expensive vol, 33% failure rate | **Reject** |
| **Hold 3.5%, do nothing** | Maintains exposure, avoids speculation | Still exposed to double disappointment at 3.5% | **Acceptable** |
| **Trim to 2.5–3.0%** | Reduces binary risk, preserves core thesis | Misses upside if confirmed | **Preferred** |
| **Add post-earnings** | Confirmed thesis, lower vol, better entry | Miss the +10-15% if it confirms | **Best risk-adjusted** |
| **Buy OTM call spread (small)** | Defined risk, participates in upside | Expensive vol, likely loses on inline result | **Acceptable if sized <0.3%** |
| **Sell put spread** | Collects premium from elevated vol | Adds downside exposure — wrong direction | **Reject** |

### Recommended Approach

**Pre-earnings (now through T-1)**:
- **Trim existing position from 3.5% to 2.5–3.0%**. Rationale: at 3.5%, a -15% move costs -53 bps. At 2.5%, it costs -38 bps. The 15 bps of reduced risk is worth more than the forgone upside given the probability distribution.
- If the analyst has high conviction, allow a **small OTM call spread** (0.2–0.3% of portfolio notional) to express the view with defined risk. Accept that elevated vol makes this expensive — it is the cost of speculating with guardrails.

**Post-earnings**:
- If earnings confirm the read-through: add aggressively (3.5% → 5.5% or higher). Vol will compress, the thesis is validated, and you are sizing on information, not speculation. Yes, you pay +10-15% more for the stock — but you are buying confirmed fundamentals, not a 67% probability.
- If earnings disappoint: reassess the thesis entirely. Do not average down into a broken read-through.

### When Pre-Event Adds ARE Appropriate

For future reference, the conditions that justify adding ahead of a binary catalyst:

1. Stock has **not moved** on the read-through (signal not yet priced)
2. Implied vol is **at or below** historical norms (cheap optionality)
3. Historical confirmation rate is **>80%** with a meaningful sample (n>10)
4. Position size after the add still allows for a **full downside scenario** without breaching risk limits
5. The add is **small** relative to the planned post-confirmation add (e.g., 0.5% now, 2% after)

This situation fails conditions 1, 2, 3, and 5.

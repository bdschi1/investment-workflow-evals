# Golden Answer — Merger Model Synergy Sensitivity (IndustrialCo / ControlsSoft)

## Deal Mechanics (Year 3, illustrative base)

All figures in $M unless noted. Tax rate 23%. Cash-funded portion =
$378M (70% of $540M); new debt = $162M (30%) at 5.0% pre-tax.

**Pro forma adjustments to standalone IndustrialCo net income (Year 3):**

| Line | Pre-tax ($M) | After-tax @ 23% ($M) |
|---|---:|---:|

| (-) Foregone interest on $378M cash @ 4.0% | (15.1) | (11.6) |
| (-) New debt interest $162M @ 5.0% | (8.1) | (6.2) |
| (-) Intangible amortization | (18.0) | (13.9) |
| (+) Cost synergies @ 100% phase (Year 3) | +15.0 | +11.6 |
| (+) Revenue synergies @ 70% phase (Year 3) | +7.0 | +5.4 |
| **Net pro forma adjustment** | | **+13.3** |

Standalone IndustrialCo net income = 250M shares x $2.40 EPS = $600M.
Combined Year-3 net income = $600M + $13.3M = **$613.3M**. Shares are
unchanged (no stock consideration). Pro forma EPS = $2.453. Accretion
vs $2.40 = **+$0.053, or +2.2%** at the base case.

## 4x2 Sensitivity Grid — Year 3 EPS Accretion / (Dilution)

Rows: synergy realization (cost + revenue, applied to announced
run-rates). Columns: revenue-synergy timing as announced vs 12-month
slip (Year-3 phase becomes 40% instead of 70%).

Each cell re-flexes only the synergy terms from the table above.
Illustrative after-tax synergy swings at Year 3:
- 100% realization, as announced = cost $11.6M + revenue $5.4M = $17.0M
- 12-month slip at 100% = cost $11.6M + revenue $3.1M = $14.7M

| Realization | As-announced timing | 12-month slip |
|---|---:|---:|

Every cell in the 4x2 grid is accretive at the stated assumptions,
because the non-synergy items (target net income less financing cost
less amortization) already add roughly $(28.0 - 11.6 - 6.2 - 13.9) =
$(-3.7)M after tax — i.e., modestly dilutive before synergies — and
even partial synergy capture closes that gap.

## Synergy Breakeven

The deal turns dilutive at Year 3 when combined after-tax synergies
fall below $3.7M. That equals about **22% of announced combined
synergies** (run-rate $25M pre-tax x 0.77 phase weighting x 0.23 tax
= ~$4.4M; breakeven realization ~22%). In round terms: if management
captures less than ~one-quarter of what was announced, the deal prints
as Year-3 EPS-dilutive.

## Commentary for the CEO (3 sentences)

This analysis shows the deal is likely Year-3 EPS accretive across the
realization and timing scenarios we tested, with a wide breakeven
cushion (dilution requires capturing less than roughly a quarter of
announced synergies). It does **not** tell you whether those synergies
will actually be realized — that is a management-execution question
that accretion math cannot answer — and it does not capture integration
cost overruns, customer-revenue churn from the transition, or the
opportunity cost of the $378M cash use. Treat the numeric outputs as
a range of scenarios contingent on synergy assumptions, not as a point
forecast of FY-out EPS.

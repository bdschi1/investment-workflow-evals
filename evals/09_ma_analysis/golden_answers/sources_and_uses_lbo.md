# Golden Answer — Sources & Uses, [COMPANY] Take-Private

Structure and mechanics that the rubric grades. SMB-placeholders
resolved to illustrative mid-cap figures below for clarity; an
actual Excel submission would use live SMB values.

## Illustrative Base Case

- [COMPANY] unaffected price: $30.00; diluted shares: 60M
- Offer price = $30.00 x 1.30 = $39.00/sh
- Equity purchase price = $39.00 x 60M = $2,340M
- Existing debt to refinance: $600M
- Existing cash: $150M; $10M min remains on BS; $140M of cash is used
- LTM EBITDA: $300M
- Senior secured term loan: 3.0x EBITDA = $900M at SOFR+375
- Sub notes: 1.25x EBITDA = $375M at 8.25% fixed
- Management rollover: 7.5% of pro-forma equity

## Methodology Notes

- Diluted shares use treasury stock method for in-the-money options;
  cash to fund options cashout above strike is a use.
- Existing debt is refinanced at close, not assumed — shown as a use.
- Advisory fee is on total enterprise value (equity purchase + refi
  debt), financing fee is on new debt raised, legal/other is a flat
  assumption.
- Management rollover is both a source (equity) and an offset to the
  sponsor equity check; it does not appear in uses.
- Minimum $10M cash post-close is assumption; anything above that is
  freed cash that reduces the sponsor check.

## Commentary

The sources-and-uses view is point-in-time at close. The 4.25x leverage
profile and 29% debt/capital mix are illustrative of a mid-cap PE
take-private structure. This table does not speak to underwriting
returns, covenant headroom, or whether the business services target can
service $1,275M of debt through an economic downturn — those live in
the LBO returns model and the debt-servicing analysis, respectively.

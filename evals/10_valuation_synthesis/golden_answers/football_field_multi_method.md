## 5. Key Observations

- The four methods span roughly $23-$46 per share. Midpoints cluster
  between $28 and $39, suggesting the valuation is not wildly
  anchored on any single method.
- Precedent transactions print the highest range, as expected, because
  comparable M&A multiples embed a control premium (20-40% over
  unaffected trading value). Weighting this method equally with the
  others would overstate intrinsic value.
- DCF and trading comps overlap heavily in the $28-$37 range; where
  two independent methods agree, the weighted midpoint becomes more
  informative than any single-method number.
- 52-week trading range is included for completeness but reflects
  market sentiment, not intrinsic or control value; it gets the
  smallest weight.
- The weighted midpoint of roughly $34/sh is a point estimate from a
  scenario-weighted spread, not a target price; the range between
  $28 and $39 is probably a more honest way to communicate the view.

## Constraints Satisfied

- Consistent net debt and share count in every bridge row.
- Net debt subtracted (not added) when going from EV to equity.
- Precedents weighted higher than 52-week but below DCF, reflecting
  control-premium caveat.
- Current share price shown on the chart as a reference line.

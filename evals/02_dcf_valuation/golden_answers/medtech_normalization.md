## Investment Thesis

### Separating Cyclical from Structural: The Core Framework

The margin recovery narrative for SDC blends two fundamentally different phenomena, and a credible valuation must decompose them:

**Cyclical Component (Volume/Utilization Recovery)**
- Procedure volumes at 85% of pre-pandemic levels represent a ~15% volume shortfall
- The margin bridge attributes -4% to volume impact (operating deleverage)
- Volume recovery is largely a function of hospital staffing and patient willingness -- factors outside SDC's control
- Recovery probability is moderate-to-high (65-75%) over 2-3 years, but the pace is uncertain
- This is mean reversion, not alpha -- it should be priced with lower uncertainty but not treated as inevitable

**Structural Component (Operational Improvement)**
- Management claims +3% from automation and +1% from SKU rationalization
- These benefits are presented as incremental to cyclical recovery, implying margins could exceed prior peaks
- However, competitors are making identical claims, which raises the question of whether these are industry table stakes or genuine differentiation
- Structural improvement should carry higher uncertainty and requires supporting evidence before it enters a base case

### What the Market May Be Missing -- In Both Directions

1. **Bulls may overestimate the structural improvement.** Management's 28% EBITDA margin target exceeds the historical peak of 26%. Achieving targets that exceed prior peaks during a period of still-depressed volumes requires extraordinary evidence. The fact that competitors claim similar improvements suggests these may reflect industry-wide cost inflation offsets rather than genuine margin expansion.

2. **Bears may underestimate the cyclical floor.** At 85% utilization, the operating deleverage is significant. Even partial volume recovery to 92-95% of pre-pandemic levels would drive 200-300bps of margin improvement with high confidence, simply from fixed cost absorption. This is the portion of the margin gap that is most likely to close.

3. **Labor dynamics are the swing factor.** The -2% labor inflation impact sits uncomfortably between cyclical and structural. Some portion will normalize as healthcare labor markets ease, but baseline wage expectations have permanently reset higher. Attributing the full -2% to either bucket would be analytically lazy.

### Supporting Evidence

- Orthopedic procedure volumes: ~90% of pre-pandemic (better than SDC overall)
- Hospital staffing shortages: persisting but improving in some regions
- Patient willingness for elective procedures: returning, constrained by scheduling capacity
- Peer company volume trends: mixed, with some reporting 92-95% recovery

## Valuation Framework

### Where to Allocate Uncertainty

The critical methodological question is not what normalized margins are, but *where in the DCF model uncertainty about normalization should reside*. There are three valid approaches, each with trade-offs:

**Option A: Reflect in Forecasts (Recommended)**
- Model multiple margin scenarios explicitly
- Assign probabilities to each path
- Do not embed a single "normalized" margin into Year 3+ projections
- Advantage: Most transparent; allows updating as evidence arrives
- Disadvantage: Requires more work and judgment

**Option B: Reflect in Terminal Value Assumptions**
- Use a range of terminal margins (20-26%) rather than a point estimate
- Weight the terminal value by probability of each margin outcome
- Advantage: Isolates long-term uncertainty from near-term forecasts
- Disadvantage: Terminal value already drives 60-70% of DCF; adding more uncertainty there compounds sensitivity

**Option C: Reflect in Discount Rate**
- Apply a higher WACC (100-200bps premium) to account for margin uncertainty
- Advantage: Simple to implement
- Disadvantage: Blunt instrument; does not distinguish between types of risk; theoretically inappropriate for company-specific operational uncertainty

**Recommended Approach:** Option A as primary, supplemented by sensitivity tables around terminal margin assumptions. The discount rate should reflect systematic risk, not management execution uncertainty.

### Evidence Requirements for Margin Assumption Upgrades

Before upgrading normalized margin assumptions beyond cyclical recovery, an analyst should require:

1. **Two consecutive quarters of margin expansion** that exceed what volume recovery alone would explain
2. **Automation capex translating to measurable productivity gains** (units per labor hour, defect rates, throughput)
3. **SKU rationalization showing in gross margin** -- not just revenue mix, but demonstrable cost removal
4. **Competitor results diverging** -- if all peers show similar improvement, it is industry/cycle, not company-specific structural alpha

## Key Risks

### 1. Volume Recovery Stalls Below Pre-Pandemic Levels
- **Probability:** 25-30%
- **Impact:** Margins stuck at 19-20%; stock at $35-40
- **Mitigation:** Monitor quarterly procedure volume data; SDC's orthopedic mix may recover faster than overall elective procedures

### 2. Management's Structural Claims Prove Aspirational
- **Probability:** 35-45%
- **Impact:** Margins normalize to 22-23% (cyclical only) rather than 26-28%; valuation discount to peers claiming execution
- **Mitigation:** Require evidence-based confirmation before embedding structural improvement in models; watch for sequential margin progression

### 3. Labor Cost Inflation Persists
- **Probability:** 30-40%
- **Impact:** 100-200bps of permanent margin headwind vs. history
- **Mitigation:** This is an industry-wide risk; relative positioning matters more than absolute margin level

### 4. Competitor Margin Claims All Materialize
- **Probability:** 20-25%
- **Impact:** If everyone achieves structural improvement, it reprices the industry -- but does not create relative advantage for SDC
- **Mitigation:** Focus on SDC's relative margin trajectory vs. peer group, not absolute targets

### 5. Robotics Adoption Slower Than Modeled
- **Probability:** 25-35%
- **Impact:** Mix shift toward higher-margin robotic procedures takes longer; margin recovery delayed
- **Mitigation:** Track robotic procedure volumes as percentage of total; hospital capital budgets for robotics platforms

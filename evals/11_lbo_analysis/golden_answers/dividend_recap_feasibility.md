# Golden Answer — Dividend Recap Feasibility (PlatformCo)

## 1. Leverage Headroom

Current LTM EBITDA = $110M. Market cap at 4.0x total leverage = $440M
of permissible total debt. Sub notes in place = $80M; available senior
envelope = $440M − $80M = **$360M senior**. Existing senior TL =
$180M; headroom for new senior debt in the recap = **$180M**. If the
recap uses a fresh senior TL that refinances the existing $180M, the
total new TL draw is $360M (refinance $180M + $180M incremental).

Post-recap structure, pre-fees:
- New senior TL: $360M
- Sub notes: $80M
- Total debt: $440M ⇒ **4.0x total leverage** on LTM EBITDA
- Senior leverage: $360M / $110M = **3.27x senior**
  (above the 3.0x soft guide; likely allowable at 4.0x total in
  market but flag to investment committee)

## 3. Impact on Returns (illustrative, 3-year-ahead exit)

Exit assumptions: Year-4 exit EBITDA = $160M (midpoint of $150-175M);
exit multiple = 10.0x; exit EV = $1,600M. Entry sponsor equity check
at original close = $200M (illustrative).

**Without recap:**
- Exit net debt ≈ $200M (further deleveraging over remaining hold)
- Exit equity = $1,600M − $200M = $1,400M
- MOIC = $1,400M / $200M = **7.0x** on entry equity
- IRR (XIRR over ~5.5 year hold) ≈ **38-40%**

**With recap:**
- Dividend of $171M at year 2.5 returns 85% of sponsor equity
- Exit net debt increases modestly (recap adds debt, partially paid
  down by year 4)
- Exit equity ≈ $1,280M (recapped structure)
- Total cash returned = $171M (recap) + $1,280M (exit) = $1,451M
- MOIC = $1,451M / $200M = **7.25x**
- IRR ≈ **42-44%** (earlier cash dominates time-value-of-money)

Recap IRR uplift is ~3-4 percentage points; MOIC uplift is modest.
The main mechanism is earlier cash return, not absolute value uplift.

## 4. Downside Stress

Year-4 EBITDA drops to $90M:
- Recapped total debt ≈ $400M after some paydown
- Total leverage = $400M / $90M = **4.44x** — below a 6.5x covenant
  but above the 4.0x raise level; should not breach a typical
  midmarket covenant at 6.5-7.0x headroom.

Downside scenario does not breach covenants at illustrative numbers.
Interest coverage should also be checked: at $90M EBITDA, senior TL
interest (say SOFR 5.0% + 400 = 9.0% on avg $340M) ≈ $30M, sub notes
interest = $6.6M, total ≈ $36.6M. Coverage = $90M / $36.6M ≈ 2.5x.
Tight but not distressed.

## 5. Recommendation (3 sentences, probabilistic)

A $180M incremental debt raise is feasible within current market
leverage norms and likely supports a ~$171M sponsor dividend after
fees. The expected IRR uplift is modest (3-4 percentage points) and
depends on eventual exit EBITDA holding at or above current levels;
the main benefit is accelerated LP cash return, not absolute value
creation. In the downside EBITDA scenario, covenants remain
uncompromised but interest coverage narrows — worth watching, not a
disqualifier.

# Golden Answer — Q4 FY26 Earnings Call → Shareholder Note

## Q4 FY26 Shareholder Note

**Headline.** Management narrowed its FY27 operating-margin guidance
to a lower range, citing sticky input costs; that change is the most
important signal from the call.

**What changed vs prior guidance.** The FY27 operating-margin outlook
was tightened from "flat to up 50 basis points" (one basis point is
one-hundredth of a percent) to "flat to down 30 basis points." In plain
terms, management now expects margins to land somewhere between roughly
unchanged and modestly down, rather than unchanged to modestly up. The
shift came from input-cost pressure that is proving more persistent
than previously forecast.

**What did not change.** FY27 organic growth guidance of 3-5% was
reaffirmed, not cut. Management still expects the base business to
grow within that range.

**Productivity program.** Management announced a $1.5 billion
three-year productivity program. About 40% is expected to fall to the
bottom line, while roughly 60% will be reinvested back into the
business for marketing, capacity, and capability building. The program
is designed to help margins recover over time rather than immediately.

**Early Q1 FY27 commentary.** One specific emerging-market region,
which represents about 8% of total company revenue, started the
quarter below plan. Management was clear that the softness is
localized to that region; the remaining 92% of revenue is tracking
in line with expectations.

**What to watch.**
- Whether input-cost pressure eases in the back half of FY27 or pushes
  margin guidance lower again.
- How quickly the productivity program's 40% flow-through begins to
  show up in reported margins.
- Whether the softer trend in the roughly-8%-of-revenue region stays
  localized or spreads to adjacent markets.

The routine accounting reclassification discussed on the call has no
impact on the income statement and is not a change to the underlying
business.

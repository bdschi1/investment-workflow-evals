## A Quick Look at Our New Industrial Distributor Position

### Summary

We started a new position in a mid-cap industrial distributor that
we think is priced below what its long-term earnings power supports.
The position is a modest 2.5% of the portfolio — not a big bet, but
big enough to matter if we're right.

### The Three Numbers That Matter

- **Expected return: roughly 18% over about two years.** This is a
  base-case estimate, not a promise. The actual return could be
  higher or lower depending on how the business performs and what
  the market pays for it.
- **Valuation: the stock trades at about 11.5 times our estimate of
  through-the-cycle earnings, compared with a peer group at about
  13.5 times.** "Through-the-cycle" means we average earnings over a
  typical economic up-and-down period, so a single bad quarter does
  not define the value.
- **Position size: 2.5% of the portfolio.** If this investment lost
  half its value, the overall portfolio impact would be roughly 1.3
  percentage points, which is a manageable loss.

### What Could Go Right

- **Peer re-rating.** If industrial sentiment stabilizes, the market
  may close the roughly two-turn valuation gap to peers without the
  business itself needing to improve.
- **Private-label and tuck-in acquisitions.** The company has a track
  record of small acquisitions at 6-7 times earnings that lift margin
  mix over time.

### What Could Go Wrong

- **Customer concentration.** About 22% of revenue comes from the
  top-five customers. If one of them in-sources or switches
  suppliers, near-term revenue takes a visible hit.
- **Working-capital pressure in a soft macro.** In a slower economy
  the company may need to hold more inventory or wait longer to be
  paid, which uses cash the business would otherwise deploy.
- **Open DOJ civil inquiry.** The Department of Justice has opened a
  civil investigative demand — a formal information request, not an
  indictment or charge — into a small ($12M in revenue) distribution
  business the company acquired in 2022. The inquiry could end with
  no action, a modest fine, or forced changes to how that small unit
  operates. It is too early to quantify the outcome.

### Bottom Line

A modest, contained position in a business that appears to be priced
cautiously for reasons we think are fixable rather than structural.
We are not recommending that any individual investor do the same
thing — portfolio fit, time horizon, and tax situation vary. We are
sharing what we did and why, in plain language.
